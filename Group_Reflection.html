Matt



Nicole

What went well?

The fact that we have already worked together in A2, made it a bit easier for co-ordinating meetings because we just pretty much kept the same days. Every team member is eager and willing to put in the effort so that is definitely a bonus. There is no tension in the group, and I believe most feel that they are heard.

What could be improved?

The content management could definitely have worked better. In A2, we all did our own thing and then loaded it up for team review near the end or in bits and pieces. This meant it was hard for us to know where everyone was at. This time we thought it would be easier to have just one document for all of us to work on. In theory, it was a good idea but in reality, not so. Editing the document on Teams was a bit harder than we anticipated and therefore we were downloading versions and editing those. Gerard was the nominated 'reviewer' and had the difficult challenge of trying to establish which version he needed to review and edit. In the end, we created a new chat called submission and stored 1 group document there. I actually think the way we did it in A2 worked better.

At least one thing that was surprising.

I really enjoyed working on the prototype and I was surprised at how much I remembered from my previous subject, User Centred Design. I think we managed to create a group with such different skills and abilities that it meant we could create an amazing prototype, webpage, video & content document.

At least one thing that you have learned about groups.

To be honest, I found working in a group very challenging. Not because of the people, they were great! I found it challenging due to the amount of time we needed to spend coordinating the project. Trying to catch up and work on things at time that suits everyone was a bit tricky. I also had to learn to step back and leave it to others and wait for them. That was a challenge for me but a good lesson to learn.
